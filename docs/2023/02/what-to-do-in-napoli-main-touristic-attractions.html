Touristic attractions in Naples

The historic center of Napoli is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is full of beautiful architecture, narrow streets, and bustling piazzas. You can visit the Royal Palace, the Castel Nuovo, and the Naples Cathedral, among other landmarks.

Quartieri Spagnoli

Quartieri Spagnoli is a historic and vibrant neighborhood located in the heart of Napoli, Italy. The neighborhood was established in the 16th century to house Spanish soldiers who were stationed in the area. Today, it is known for its narrow streets, colorful buildings, and lively atmosphere. Quartieri Spagnoli is a great place to experience the authentic culture of Naples and to get a taste of traditional Neapolitan life. Visitors can explore the local markets, try the delicious street food, and chat with the friendly locals. The neighborhood is also home to several historic landmarks, including the church of Santa Maria degli Angeli and the Castel Sant‚ÄôElmo.


Via dei Tribunali

Via dei Tribunali is a historic street in the heart of Napoli, Italy. The street is known for its narrow lanes, traditional shops, and historic landmarks. It was named after the courts and tribunals that were once located in the area. Today, the street is a popular destination for tourists and locals alike, who come to enjoy the traditional Neapolitan atmosphere and sample the delicious street food. Some of the most popular dishes include pizza, sfogliatelle, and arancini. Along the street, visitors can find several historic landmarks, including the church of San Lorenzo Maggiore and the Naples Underground.


Lungomare di Napoli

The Lungomare di Napoli is a scenic promenade that stretches for several kilometers along the Bay of Naples, offering stunning views of the sea and the imposing Mount Vesuvius in the distance. It is a popular spot for both locals and tourists, who come to go for a leisurely stroll or bike ride, or simply sit and take in the beautiful scenery.

Along the Lungomare, visitors can find several historic landmarks and attractions, including the Castel dell‚ÄôOvo, a castle that dates back to the 12th century, and the Santa Lucia neighborhood, which is known for its vibrant nightlife and seafood restaurants. The Lungomare is also home to several beautiful parks, including the Villa Comunale and the Parco Virgiliano, which offer a peaceful escape from the hustle and bustle of the city.

At night, the Lungomare transforms into a lively gathering place, with street performers, live music, and outdoor cafes and bars. During the summer months, it is a popular spot for swimming and sunbathing, with several beach clubs and lidos lining the waterfront. Overall, the Lungomare di Napoli is a must-visit attraction in Naples, offering a perfect combination of natural beauty, history, and local culture.


Piazza del Plebiscito

Piazza del Plebiscito is one of the most impressive and largest public squares in Naples, located in the heart of the city. The square was named after the plebiscite that occurred in Naples in 1860, which led to the unification of Italy. The square has a long and fascinating history and is surrounded by many important buildings and monuments.

At the center of the Piazza del Plebiscito stands the magnificent Basilica of San Francesco di Paola, a church built in the neoclassical style with a large dome and two symmetrical colonnades. The church was completed in 1816 and is modeled after the Pantheon in Rome. The Basilica is open to the public and visitors can enjoy the stunning interior of the church, which is adorned with beautiful frescoes and sculptures.

The Royal Palace of Naples, a former residence of the Bourbon kings, is located on the east side of the square. The palace was built in the 17th century and is a stunning example of Baroque architecture. Today, the palace houses the National Library of Naples and the Museum of the Royal Apartments. The Royal Palace‚Äôs ornate facade is impressive, with its intricate carvings and detailed reliefs, and the interior is just as grand, with lavish rooms and opulent decorations.

Overall, Piazza del Plebiscito is a must-visit attraction in Naples, offering a perfect combination of stunning architecture, history, and culture. Visitors can stroll around the square, admire the impressive buildings and monuments, and soak up the atmosphere of this vibrant public space.

The Galleria Umberto I is an iconic shopping arcade that was built in the late 19th century and is located in the heart of Naples. It is a stunning example of neoclassical architecture, with ornate arches, marble floors, and beautiful frescoes on the ceilings. The Galleria is home to some of the most high-end boutiques and shops in Naples and is a popular destination for tourists, shoppers, and locals.

The Galleria Umberto I is not only a great place to go shopping but also a popular spot for tourists exploring the historic center of Naples. Cappella Sansevero

The Cappella Sansevero, also known as the Chapel of the Veiled Christ, is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Naples, Italy. Located in the historic center of the city, the chapel is a masterpiece of Baroque art and architecture. The chapel was commissioned by the Prince of Sansevero in the 18th century, and it is now open to the public as a museum.

The highlight of the chapel is the famous Veiled Christ, a sculpture that is considered one of the most remarkable works of art in the world. The sculpture was created by Giuseppe Sanmartino in the 18th century and depicts the body of Christ draped in a thin veil. The sculpture is so detailed and lifelike that it appears as though the veil is actually draped over a real body. The Veiled Christ is a masterpiece of art and is widely regarded as one of the greatest sculptures of all time.

In addition to the Veiled Christ, the chapel is also home to many other impressive works of art, including the Anatomical Machines, a series of sculptures that depict the human body in intricate detail. Visitors to the chapel can also explore the many other works of art on display, including paintings, sculptures, and frescoes. The Cappella Sansevero is a unique and fascinating destination that should not be missed by anyone traveling to Naples.
